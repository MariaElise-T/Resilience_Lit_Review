The purpose of this study was to verify the mediating effect of resilience in the relationship between depression and academic burnout perceived by female college students. A survey was conducted on female college students in the metropolitan area and non-metropolitan areas on depression, academic burnout, and resilience, and 178 copies of the collected data were analyzed. The results of correlation analysis and hierarchical regression analysis using SPSS 18.0 are as follows. First, there was a positive correlation between depression and academic burnout, a negative correlation between depression and resilience, and a negative correlation between resilience and academic burnout. Second, resilience showed a complete mediating effect in the relationship between depression perceived by female college students and academic burnout. The significance of the results of this study is that it provided basic data to present the direction and contents of guidance or counseling programs to effectively deal with academic burnout of college students.
