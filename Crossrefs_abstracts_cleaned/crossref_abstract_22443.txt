The purpose of this study is to examine the effect of client violence on post traumatic stress of psychiatric hospital workers and to verify the mediating effect of resilience on this relationship. For this purpose, descriptive statistics and regression analysis were conducted based on the results of a survey conducted on 772 people working in mental health institutions in Daegu and Gyeongsangbuk-do to analyze the contents. Results First, it was found that client violence experienced by psychiatric hospital workers had a significant effect on post-traumatic stress. Second, the resilience of workers in psychiatric institutions was found to act as a partial mediating variable between client violence and post-traumatic stress. Based on the results of this study, a practical strategy for reducing post-traumatic stress caused by client violence in psychiatric hospital workers and a plan to improve resilience were proposed.
