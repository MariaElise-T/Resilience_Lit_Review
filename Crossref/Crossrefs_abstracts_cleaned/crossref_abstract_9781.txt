Cancer patients generally have families who provide some level of care. The family or family caregiver has a big task and role in serving the needs of the patient, both physically and psychologically. The role and level of these responsibilities can lead to a high burden or pressure felt by the family caregiver, which is called the caregiver burden. This training targets resilience as a variable that can reduce the caregiver burden of family caregivers. The research design is a one group pretest and posttest experimental design. The number of participants who took part in the entire training process was four people. The results of the different test showed a significance of 0.144 (p > 0.05), which means that there was no significant difference in the caregiver burden level after the training.
